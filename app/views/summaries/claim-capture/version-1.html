First iteration

In this prototype, we were looking at how agents might handle capturing a new claim.

Research notes

Agents arriving on the find a claim landing page automatically used the find feature rather than looking at the other options available to them. This caused confusion as the claim is pre-populated for testing purposes.

The paused claim banner was missed by some agents and some did not notice it until they were a few pages into setting up the new claim. This lead some agents to believe that this banner was in relation to the current claim and a way of informing them that they shouldn't progress any further with this claim as it would be disallowed.

The international agents that we tested with were unsure as to why a disallowed claim would be built in the system as claimants are discouraged from applying if they do not meet the eligibility criteria. This behaviour is expected to be slightly different at Dover as the team work in a different way.

The form asks if the claimant address is the address that they lived at when their partner died, as a response to this agents were looking at the partner details on the form and then presuming it was the same address.

In the scenario where the claimant had stated that they were married on the claim form but hadn't provided a certificate the agents were unsure about how to answer, some were dubious about taking the word of the claimant, especially as their name is listed next to the verification/pause action.

There was also confusion around whether or not the claimant had dependant children when they stated they were pregnant on the claim form. This is a rare scenario compared to the general claim volumes so this may be causing some of the uncertainty.
