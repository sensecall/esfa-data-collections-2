First iteration

In this prototype, we were looking at how agents might handle the processing of claims. The prototype looks at pulling a claim from a queue and trying to verify the evidence to put the claim into payment.

Research notes

This sparked a lot of positive feedback from users regarding the simplicity and ease of use of the feature as a whole.

The terms 'capture' and 'process' caused some confusion as this is unfamiliar terminology. Bereavement Service claims don't require verification for date of death so agents weren't sure what they would do under those circumstances but agents did raise that they would need to see a date of death to know what they are verifying, especially if they had not been responsible for inputting the claim.

It was presumed that the claimant did have dependent children as agents were asked if they could verify the Child Benefit, however agents do not pause cases under these circumstances, they would pay the standard rate until the claimant informed them of a change of circumstances.

Agents were not sure what they would do if a claimant was not married or didn't have enough National Insurance contributions as the only option presented was to pause these claims on a loop rather than disallow. The pause options provided were appreciated by agents as they were the more common time periods used during processing, however, if a case needed to be referred to the Relationship Verification Unit or a text message had been sent the time periods could vary hugely and agents need this to be accounted for.

When viewing a case that had been previously paused the log was useful as agents could be sure who had verified certain pieces of information and when.

When processing a claim agents weren't always aware that they had started working on a different claim, they expected to be able to see the previous one again.
