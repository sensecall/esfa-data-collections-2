BPS-912

Clerical to system

Wording around 'have all payments been made up to yesteday'.

Changes made

Prototype designed to test the clerical to system process again. In this version the question has been changed to 'Have all payments up to and including yesterday been made?'

The validation messages have also been changed. Enter 8 digits into the account number field to activate the error messages.

Research notes

The question asking if payments were up to date as of yesterday didn't cause any problems for agents during testing and again the question was audibly read aloud compared to previous versions.

Agents were aware that any extra information they needed regarding previous payments would be in DRS, CPS or CAMlite as the front sheet would have been uploaded, therefore the payment information displayed on the schedule was enough to initially answer payment queries. Agents were reassured by the planned payments being shown on the schedule as they had confidence that the future payments would go out as planned.

Bank account validation was recognised by agents as 'hooking in' to another system to verify the account details provided. Some agents asked before setting up the payment schedule if it would validate any bank details so their expectations were met when the error message had been received.

Standard to Higher

Agents were uncertain as to how to change the payment rate from standard to higher, this is as the change button was viewed as only being applicable to bank details rather than the full table it was situated above. Once the change had been applied agents were surprised that the system had worked out the arrears and no manual calculations or payments were required to pay the claimant what they were owed.

Duplication

The guidance provided was not used, agents confidently navigated around the prototype to find and view the schedule and from this knew that the claim could be a duplicate or a genuine second claim for a different spouse, but if this was the case the second claim would need to be paid clerically. Agents were curious as to whether the clerical to system option could be used for an individual with two claims to migrate the second to the system once the first had finished the schedule.
